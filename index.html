Research Interests

Broadly: how do we teach AI systems the cultural and aesthetic judgment that humans use when working with creative artifacts — translated fiction, music, film, and beyond?

Literary Machine Translation & Cultural Adaptation

My main focus. MT systems are getting linguistically good but remain culturally flat — they skip the adaptation decisions that human translators spend most of their energy on. I'm interested in whether cultural mediation (the judgment layer, not the bilingual layer) can be separated out and taught through reinforcement learning and preference feedback. Starting with Japanese-English, where I have the most domain knowledge.

Language-Heavy Fiction as a Stress Test

The fiction I care about most is work where language is the art — Monogatari's wordplay, Joyce's linguistic invention, visual novels with puns as plot mechanics. These are the hardest cases for translation (human or machine) because culture and language are completely entangled. I think they're also where the most interesting research questions live.

Cross-Cultural Aesthetic Recommendation

A secondary thread: can taste in one creative domain help you discover things in another, across cultural boundaries? Currently exploring how mood and genre categories in music map differently across Japanese, American, and Brazilian listening contexts. Longer term, interested in connecting this to literary discovery — helping people find fiction from traditions they'd never otherwise encounter.

Preference Learning & Reward Modeling

The technical thread tying everything together. DPO, RLHF, and reward models are proving effective for aligning language models — I want to apply these tools to domains where "quality" is subjective and culturally conditioned rather than absolute.

Current Projects

Cultural Adaptation Error Analysis for Japanese-English Literary MT

Comparing professional human translations of Japanese fiction against outputs from GPT-4, Claude, and TranslateGemma. Building a taxonomy of cultural adaptation failure modes — reference flattening, register collapse, pragmatic mistranslation, tonal drift — and examining whether these errors are systematic, predictable by passage type, and invisible to standard automatic metrics (COMET, MetricX, BERTScore).

literary MT evaluation cultural adaptation in progress

Cross-Cultural Aesthetic Mapping in Music

Using Spotify's public API to analyze how mood and genre descriptors (e.g., "nostalgic," "melancholic," "energetic") correspond to different acoustic profiles across Japanese, American, and Brazilian listening contexts. Testing whether categories that recommendation systems treat as universal are actually culturally particular.
